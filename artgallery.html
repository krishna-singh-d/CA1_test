Mona Lisa

By Leonardo da Vinci

Ouick link
  • Mona Lisa

    • The Mona Lisa is arguably the most famous painting in the world. It is a half-length portrait
    painting by the Italian Renaissance artist Leonardo da Vinci.
    Interesting Fact: The painting was stolen in 1911 and recovered two years later.
    *It is an oil painting on a poplar panel.*

    The Starry Night

    By Vincent van Gogh

    Ouick link
  • The Starry Night

    • This *oil on canvas* painting depicts the view from Van Gogh's asylum room
    at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence.
    It is now one of his most recognized pieces.

    The Persistence of Memory

    By Salvador Dalí

    Ouick link
  • The Persistence of Memory

    • A key work in Surrealism, this small painting is recognizable by its melting pocket watches.
    It was first exhibited in 1932. Dalí famously associated the melting clocks with *softness* and the relativity of time.

    Guernica

    By Pablo Picasso

    Ouick link
  • Guernica

    • Guernica is a massive *oil painting on canvas* created in 1937.
    It is an artistic response to the bombing of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War.
    Source: The Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía in Madrid, Spain.

    The Scream

    By Edvard Munch

    Ouick link
  • The Scream

    • The Scream (*Skrik*) is the popular name for several versions by *Expressionist* artist Edvard Munch.
    It depicts a figure with an agonizing expression against a landscape with a dramatic orange sky.
